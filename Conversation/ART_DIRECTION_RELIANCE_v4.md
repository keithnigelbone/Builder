# ART DIRECTION — RELIANCE VISUAL LANGUAGE

v4

Upload this file before any prompt-writing session. It tells Claude how to see, frame, light, and describe every image — grounded in Reliance's brand expression, principles, and visual identity.

## THE BRAND EXPRESSION

Grounded Confidence.

Clarity, precision, quiet authority. Not loudness. Not spectacle. Confidence earned through doing — building, operating, moving forward. Every image must embody this. Every prompt must produce it.

Three lenses shape this expression. Every image honours at least one. The strongest honour all three.

**Authority & Respect**
Refinement over statement. Sharp edges, controlled geometry, stable visual language. The subject commands the frame. The composition is considered. Nothing is accidental. — Strong geometric framing. Precise directional light. Subject has presence and mass. Environment is contextual, not decorative.

**Scalability & Growth**
Scale without losing the human. A worker against a vast solar field. A single car at a charging bay that implies a city. The part that represents the whole. — Environmental context recedes into the image. Infrastructure extends beyond the frame. The subject is never isolated.

**Care**
Detail over decoration. Every element earns its place. Light is deliberate. Hands are doing something real. Location is specific. — Hands always engaged. Clothing correct for the role. Nothing in the frame is accidental or unresolved.

## THE 7 PRINCIPLES IN IMAGERY

Not captions. The reason an image exists.

**We care** — Show people operating with consequence. The scale of the field, the precision of the work, the environment surrounding the action. Long-term thinking visible in the frame.

**Customer value** — Show the moment value is delivered. A hand on a charging cable. A freshly installed panel. The benefit visible, not implied.

**Excellence** — Sharp, considered, uncompromised. No soft focus where clarity is needed. No generic framing where a specific one exists. Mediocrity is never passed on — including in a prompt.

**One team** — Where multiple people appear, they share a direction. No hierarchy in the frame. Every contribution recognised.

**Ownership mindset** — The individual whose work connects to something national, generational, environmental. The person is not small. The context is large.

**Respect** — The worker at the valve is shot with the same precision as the executive. Eye level. Slight low angle. Never looking down on a subject.

**Integrity** — Never staged, never stock, never artificial. Real skin tones, real locations, real light. What you see is what the work looks like.

## THE VISUAL BASELINE

Every prompt closes with this. Exactly as written. No exceptions.

> eye level shot, warm golden directional light, bright on subject with rich shadow detail preserved, high colour saturation, bokeh background depth, intimate push-in framing, mid-to-high brightness, authentic Indian skin tones, vivid festive or natural colours, no artificial fill, cinematic documentary feel

For aerial / top-down only:

> true top-down aerial perspective, warm golden directional light raking across surfaces, high colour saturation, vivid [scene-specific] colours, mid-to-high brightness, no artificial fill, cinematic documentary feel

## THREE RULES THAT NEVER BREAK

1. **Light has a direction.** Sun low. One side. Raking across faces, surfaces, land. Shadows are long and rich — not eliminated. Nothing flat. Nothing even.
2. **Bright on subject. Shadow detail preserved.** The subject is lit. Shadows are not crushed to black. Texture reads in dark areas — fabric, skin, metal, earth. Presence, not overexposure.
3. **Bokeh depth. Intimate push-in.** Camera close. Something soft anchors the foreground. Background separates but does not disappear. Subject has context. The image breathes.

## LIGHT

**What works**

- warm golden directional light — default. Sun low and to one side. Always name the direction.
- dappled backlight through trees — agriculture, field, forest. Light breaks through canopy. Warm and organic.
- crisp cool diffused light, no directional warmth — interiors, clean factories, data centres, autonomous environments. Razor sharp. No haze.
- vibrant LED blue indicator lights — server racks, robotic systems, warehouse machinery. Used as a deliberate contrast accent against cool white or warm amber environments. Never the dominant light source. Always a secondary detail that adds depth and technological presence.
- 80% crisp cool facility light, 20% warm golden ambient — internal warehouses and fulfilment centres only. The space reads as clean and modern but is not sterile or cold. The warm 20% enters from one side — a high window, a loading bay, an open door — and rakes gently across the floor or machinery surfaces. It prevents the interior from feeling flooded with flat light. The cool dominates. The warm grounds it.
- soft diffused ambient light, no direct source — detail shots, hands, fabric, texture-led frames only.

**What kills a prompt**

- dramatic lighting — theatre, not documentary
- soft light alone — produces nothing
- natural light alone — too vague
- fill light or studio flash — never, unless hard editorial is explicitly required

## FRAMING

**People**

- Medium close-up, slight low angle. Subject has presence.
- Both hands doing something. Operating, tending, turning, connecting. Never resting.
- Subject offset in frame. Body language reads.
- Foreground element soft and out of focus. Panel edge, leaf, pipe. Anchors the image.
- Background bokeh — present, not competing.

**No people — machines, infrastructure, aerials**

- True top-down or wide low angle. Never tilted.
- Wide enough to show scale. Infrastructure fills the frame.
- Earth or environment at edges. Context, not void.
- Light rakes across surfaces from one side — even from above, light has direction.

**Never**

- Subject looking at camera and smiling (unless explicitly requested)
- Centred symmetrical composition
- Tilted aerial
- Empty foreground with no anchor

## COLOUR BY SCENE

**Agriculture — Care made visible**
Patient, generational work. Deep greens, ochre earth, red-brown soil. Hands in contact with the crop. Dappled directional light. Scale of the field present but human detail never lost.

**Solar energy — Scalability made real**
Clean geometry against Indian earth. Steel blue panels in ordered rows, red-brown Rajasthan ground between them. The person is precise and purposeful. Infrastructure extends beyond the frame.

**Oil and gas — Authority in operation**
Industrial scale, human precision. Grey pipework, warm amber earth, flare stacks at distance. Both hands engaged — turning, adjusting, maintaining. Nothing decorative. Everything functional.

**EV / urban — Modernity with purpose**
Clean tarmac, white vehicles, warm coastal light. The person connects, charges, moves forward. City present in background — palm trees, contemporary buildings — not competing.

**Future technology — Precision at the frontier**
Clean facilities, minimal machines, considered environments. Technology held, operated, or worn by real people. No sci-fi. No CGI. No neon. Grounded confidence applied to what comes next.

**Clean factory / logistics — Scale without spectacle**
Ultra-clean environments, minimal white machines, polished surfaces. Crisp cool light for interiors. Warm golden light for exteriors. Geometric order communicates confidence. No clutter. No noise.

**Celebration — Integrity of culture**
Vivid reds, pinks, golds, warm light. Real moments of connection — an embrace, a ritual, a gesture. No staging. No performance. Colour earned by the occasion.

**People across all scenes**
Regardless of role — farmer, engineer, technician, driver — always shot with the same respect and precision. Eye level or slight low angle. Both hands doing something real. Authentic Indian skin tones. The role does not determine the dignity of the frame.

**Saturation**

- People in scene: high — colour is emotion
- Aerial / landscape: medium-high — colour shows scale
- Clean interiors / machines: medium — form and geometry carry the image

## LOCATION SPECIFICITY

India is not a backdrop. Name it. Give it physical detail.

- **Rajasthan** — red-brown earth, dry scrubland, neem trees, banyan trees, flat horizon, harsh clear sky
- **Mumbai** — palm trees, contemporary buildings, warm coastal light, clean tarmac, layered urban density
- **Agricultural India** — dense green crop plants, tall trees with full canopy, rich ochre soil, dappled light
- **Indian solar farm** — geometric rows of blue-grey panels, red-brown earth between rows, wide flat land
- **Indian oil refinery** — pipe networks, flare stacks, processing towers, warm industrial earth at perimeter
- **Indian data centre / facility** — clean white architecture, polished surfaces, ordered infrastructure, clear sky or controlled interior

Never write: *in India*, *Indian location*, *typical Indian setting* — these produce nothing specific.

## PROMPT STRUCTURE

Four parts. All four present. Always in this order.

```
[SUBJECT — physical description + clothing]
[ACTION — both hands doing something specific]
[LOCATION — named Indian place with physical detail]
[FRAMING — shot type and angle]
[VISUAL BASELINE — exactly as written above]
```

## PRINCIPLE CHECK

Run before every prompt is finalised.

| Question | If no |
|---|---|
| Does the image express Authority & Respect, Scalability & Growth, or Care? | Reframe the composition |
| Is the subject treated with the same precision regardless of role? | Adjust angle — never shoot down |
| Is the scale of the work visible? | Widen the location description |
| Are the hands doing something real and specific? | Name the action precisely |
| Is the location named with physical detail? | Replace generic with specific |
| Does the visual baseline close the prompt unchanged? | Use it exactly as written |

## BANNED WORDS

dramatic lighting, beautiful, professional photography, realistic, natural light alone, soft light alone, stunning, vibrant alone, perfect, amazing, high quality, photorealistic alone, in India, Indian setting, typical

Never write "hundreds" or "thousands" in aerial shots. Give a real number or describe infrastructure extending to the horizon.

## HARD NO — COMPETITOR BRANDS

Never reference, name, imply, or include any visual identity associated with TATA Group. This includes the TATA name, wordmark, any TATA-branded vehicle, product, or infrastructure, and any logo, colour, or brand mark associated with TATA companies — TATA Motors, TATA Power, TATA Steel, TCS, Jaguar Land Rover, or any subsidiary.

Describe all vehicles, products, and infrastructure generically. "white electric car" not a brand name. "passenger vehicle" not a marque. "steel structure" not a manufacturer.

No exceptions.
